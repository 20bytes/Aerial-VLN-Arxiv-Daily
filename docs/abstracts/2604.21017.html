Open-H-Embodiment: A Large-Scale Dataset for Enabling Foundation Models in Medical Robotics

Abstract (EN)

Autonomous medical robots hold promise to improve patient outcomes, reduce provider workload, democratize access to care, and enable superhuman precision. However, autonomous medical robotics has been limited by a fundamental data problem: existing medical robotic datasets are small, single-embodiment, and rarely shared openly, restricting the development of foundation models that the field needs to advance. We introduce Open-H-Embodiment, the largest open dataset of medical robotic video with synchronized kinematics to date, spanning more than 49 institutions and multiple robotic platforms including the CMR Versius, Intuitive Surgical's da Vinci, da Vinci Research Kit (dVRK), Rob Surgical BiTrack, Virtual Incision's MIRA, Moon Surgical Maestro, and a variety of custom systems, spanning surgical manipulation, robotic ultrasound, and endoscopy procedures. We demonstrate the research enabled by this dataset through two foundation models. GR00T-H is the first open foundation vision-language-action model for medical robotics, which is the only evaluated model to achieve full end-to-end task completion on a structured suturing benchmark (25% of trials vs. 0% for all others) and achieves 64% average success across a 29-step ex vivo suturing sequence. We also train Cosmos-H-Surgical-Simulator, the first action-conditioned world model to enable multi-embodiment surgical simulation from a single checkpoint, spanning nine robotic platforms and supporting in silico policy evaluation and synthetic data generation for the medical domain. These results suggest that open, large-scale medical robot data collection can serve as critical infrastructure for the research community, enabling advances in robot learning, world modeling, and beyond.

摘要 (ZH)

自主医疗机器人有望改善患者预后、减轻医护人员工作负担、普及医疗服务并实现超人类精度。然而，自主医疗机器人领域长期受制于一个根本性的数据问题：现有医疗机器人数据集规模小、单具身且极少公开共享，这限制了该领域发展所需的基础模型。我们提出Open-H-Embodiment，这是迄今为止规模最大的医疗机器人视频与同步运动学开放数据集，涵盖49家以上机构及多种机器人平台，包括CMR Versius、直觉外科的达芬奇、达芬奇研究套件（dVRK）、Rob Surgical BiTrack、Virtual Incision的MIRA、Moon Surgical Maestro以及多种定制系统，覆盖手术操作、机器人超声和内窥镜程序。我们通过两个基础模型展示了该数据集所推动的研究。GR00T-H是首个面向医疗机器人的开放基础视觉-语言-动作模型，它是唯一在结构化缝合基准测试中实现完整端到端任务完成的评估模型（试验成功率为25%，其他模型均为0%），并在29步离体缝合序列中平均成功率达64%。我们还训练了Cosmos-H-外科模拟器，这是首个支持从单一检查点实现多具身外科模拟的基于动作条件的世界模型，涵盖九个机器人平台，可支持医疗领域的计算机策略评估与合成数据生成。这些结果表明，开放的大规模医疗机器人数据收集可作为研究社区的关键基础设施，推动机器人学习、世界建模等领域的发展。
